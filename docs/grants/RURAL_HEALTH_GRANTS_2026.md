## 1. CMS Rural Health Transformation Program

### Overview

The Rural Health Transformation (RHT) Program is the largest rural healthcare funding initiative in U.S. history, established under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (Public Law 119-21).

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Total Funding** | $50 billion over 5 years (2026-2030) |
| **Annual Funding** | $10 billion per fiscal year |
| **State Awards Range** | $147 million - $281 million (FY2026) |
| **Average State Award** | ~$200 million |
| **Administering Agency** | Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) |

### Eligible Technology Activities

The program explicitly funds:

- **AI and Advanced Technologies**
  - Artificial intelligence for clinical decision support
  - Remote monitoring systems
  - Robotics in healthcare delivery

- **Digital Health Infrastructure**
  - Consumer-facing, technology-driven solutions
  - Chronic disease prevention and management platforms
  - Telehealth expansion and enhancement

- **IT Modernization**
  - Software and hardware for efficiency improvements
  - Cybersecurity capability development
  - Data sharing and interoperability systems

### How Technology Vendors Participate

**This is NOT a direct grant application.** States receive the funds and contract with vendors.

**Vendor participation path:**
1. Register as a vendor with target state health agencies
2. Monitor state procurement opportunities
3. Respond to RFPs issued by state health departments
4. Propose pilot programs or partnerships
5. Engage state rural health offices directly

## 2. NIA a2 Collective AI AgeTech Pilot Awards

### Overview

The a2 Collective represents the Artificial Intelligence and Technology Collaboratories (AITC) for Aging Research program, funded by the National Institute on Aging (NIA).

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Program Funding** | $40 million earmarked for AI AgeTech |
| **Award Amount** | Up to $200,000 direct costs |
| **Typical Range** | $50,000 - $150,000 |
| **Duration** | 12 months |
| **Indirect Costs** | Federally negotiated rate, or 10% max |

### Eligibility Requirements

**Who Can Apply:**
- For-profit companies ✓
- Nonprofit organizations ✓
- Higher education institutions ✓
- Government agencies ✓
- Must have Unique Entity ID (UEI) within United States

**Principal Investigator:**
- Does NOT require PhD or MD
- Must demonstrate necessary skills and knowledge

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot propose incremental enhancements to technologies previously funded by NIH SBIR/STTR

### Focus Areas

Projects must address healthy aging and/or Alzheimer's disease and related dementias (AD/ADRD) through AI and technology.

**Priority Consideration Given To:**
- Work leveraging AITC resources
- Projects generating shareable datasets/software
- Addressing health disparities
- **Improving rural access to care** ← Perfect fit

### Application Process

**Two-Round Process:**

| Round | Description | Timeline (2025 cycle) |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Round 1 | Brief proposal submission | Dec 2, 2024 - Jan 15, 2025 |
| Round 1 Decisions | | ~Feb 19, 2025 |
| Round 2 | Detailed proposal (invite-only) | Due Mar 31, 2025 |
| Round 2 Decisions | | Mid-May 2025 |

**Next Cycle:** Expected mid-to-late 2026 (contingent on NIA program renewal)

### Alignment with WellFit

| a2 Focus Area | WellFit Capability |
|---------------|-------------------|
| AI for healthy aging | Daily wellness monitoring with AI analysis |
| Older adults including dementia | Senior-focused interface design |
| Rural access to care | Low-bandwidth, distributed architecture |
| Health disparities | Underserved community focus |
| Technology for caregivers | Caregiver coordination and PIN-based access |

## 3. AHRQ Digital Healthcare Research

### Overview

The Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ) offers multiple funding opportunities for AI in healthcare and digital health solutions.

### R18: Examining the Impact of AI on Healthcare Safety

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Mechanism** | R18 Research Demonstration |
| **Due Dates** | Multiple, through May 26, 2027 |
| **Focus** | AI systems' impact on patient safety |

**Objective:** Support healthcare safety by determining whether and how breakthrough uses of AI systems affect patient safety, and how AI systems can be safely implemented.

**Alignment:** Your 40+ AI services could be studied/validated through this mechanism.

### R21/R33: Digital Healthcare Solutions at Point of Care

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Mechanism** | R21 (exploratory) / R33 (full development) |
| **Due Dates** | Multiple, through August 1, 2026 |
| **Focus** | Digital solutions improving care quality |

**Objective:** Improve quality of healthcare services delivery at the point of care through integration of digital healthcare solutions.

**Alignment:** AI-based clinical decision support, patient monitoring, care coordination.

### Key Links

- [AHRQ Digital Healthcare Funding](https://digital.ahrq.gov/ahrq-digital-healthcare-research-funding-opportunities)

## 4. HRSA Telehealth Grant Programs

### Overview

The Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) offers multiple telehealth-focused grant programs through the Federal Office of Rural Health Policy (FORHP).

### Telehealth Network Grant Program (TNGP)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Focus** | Rural tele-emergency services |
| **Emphasis** | Tele-stroke, tele-behavioral health, Tele-EMS |
| **Eligibility** | Rural or urban nonprofit providing direct clinical services |

**Note:** For-profit entities CAN participate in networks, though lead applicant must be nonprofit.

### Telehealth Nutrition Services Network (HRSA-26-076)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Total Funding** | $10,500,000 |
| **Expected Awards** | 35 |
| **Maximum Award** | $300,000 |
| **Focus** | Chronic disease prevention via telehealth nutrition |
| **Expected Posting** | December 2025 |

### Chronic Care Telehealth Centers of Excellence (HRSA-26-077)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Total Funding** | $19,000,000 |
| **Expected Awards** | 5 |
| **Maximum Award** | $3,800,000 |
| **Focus** | Academic medical centers integrating telehealth |
| **Expected Posting** | February 2026 |
| **Eligibility** | Academic medical centers, higher education institutions |

**Partnership Opportunity:** Partner with academic medical center as technology vendor.
