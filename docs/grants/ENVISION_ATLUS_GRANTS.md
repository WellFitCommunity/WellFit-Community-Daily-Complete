# PRIORITY 1: CLINICAL AI & DECISION SUPPORT

## AHRQ PA-24-261: Impact of AI on Healthcare Safety (R18)

**Agency:** Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ)
**Mechanism:** R18 (Research Demonstration)
**Amount:** Up to $1.5M total costs (varies by project scope)
**Deadline:** Standard due dates through 2027

### Program Focus
Supports research examining whether and how AI systems affect patient safety, and how AI systems can be safely implemented and used in healthcare delivery.

### Envision Atlus Alignment

| Requirement | Our Capability |
|-------------|----------------|
| AI deployment in clinical settings | 45+ production AI models in active use |
| Patient safety outcomes | Risk prediction reduces adverse events |
| Implementation strategies | Documented deployment across tenants |
| Positive/negative impact assessment | A/B testing infrastructure exists |

### Key Considerations
- **Does NOT fund new AI development** - focuses on evaluation
- Ideal for validating existing Envision Atlus AI models
- Requires clinical partner with patient data access
- Strong fit for demonstrating safety of deployed models

**Source:** [NIH PA-24-261](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PA-24-261.html)

---

## AHRQ PA-24-266: Digital Healthcare Solutions (R21/R33)

**Agency:** AHRQ
**Mechanism:** R21/R33 Phased Innovation Award
**Amount:** R21: Up to $275K/2 years; R33: Up to $500K/year for 3 years
**Deadline:** Standard due dates (typically February, June, October)

### Program Focus
Tests promising digital healthcare interventions at the point of care to improve quality of care and care delivery using:
- Advanced analytics
- Innovative patient-centered digital health technologies
- Clinician decision support tools

### Envision Atlus Alignment

| Requirement | Our Capability |
|-------------|----------------|
| Clinical decision support | Integrated CDS alerts and recommendations |
| Point-of-care interventions | Real-time risk alerts to clinicians |
| Advanced analytics | 45+ ML models, predictive analytics |
| EHR integration | FHIR R4 with 21 resource types |
| Workflow optimization | AI-based task prioritization |

### Phase Structure
- **R21 (Years 1-2):** Pilot testing, feasibility validation
- **R33 (Years 3-5):** Expanded implementation, outcomes measurement
- Transition to R33 requires demonstrating Phase 1 success

**Source:** [NIH PA-24-266](https://grants.nih.gov/grants/guide/pa-files/PA-24-266.html)

**Mechanism:** State block grant program
**Amount:** $50 billion over 5 years ($10B/year FY2026-2030)
**Timeline:** State awards began 2026

### Technology Components Funded
- Technical assistance, software, and hardware for IT advances
- Remote monitoring, robotics, AI, advanced technologies
- Cybersecurity capability development
- Care delivery technology solutions

### State-Level Engagement Strategy
Rather than direct application, engage with state health departments implementing RHT programs:

1. **Identify Texas RHT coordinator** - Texas will receive $147M-$281M
2. **Propose technology partnership** - Envision Atlus as technology vendor
3. **Focus on rural hospitals** - Technology modernization component
