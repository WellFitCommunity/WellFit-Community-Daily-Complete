## 1. USDA Distance Learning & Telemedicine (DLT) Grants

### Program Overview

**Agency:** USDA Rural Development
**Program:** Distance Learning and Telemedicine Grant Program

The DLT Program provides financial assistance to enable and improve distance learning and telemedicine services in rural areas through telecommunications-enabled equipment and technology.

### Funding Details

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Award Range** | $50,000 - $1,000,000 |
| **Match Requirement** | 15% (can be in-kind) |
| **Performance Period** | 3 years |
| **FY 2026 Deadline** | Expected Spring 2026 (check grants.gov) |

### Eligible Applicants

- Incorporated organizations (public or private)
- State or local governments
- Federally recognized tribes
- Consortia of eligible entities

**Key Requirement:** Must serve rural areas with populations ≤20,000

### Eligible Uses

| Allowed | Not Allowed |
|---------|-------------|
| Video conferencing equipment | Salaries |
| Remote monitoring devices | Operating costs |
| Telemedicine carts | Construction |
| Software/applications | Land acquisition |
| Installation costs | Vehicles |
| Technical training | Recurring service fees |

### Why This Fits WellFit

| WellFit Capability | DLT Alignment |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Telehealth platform | Video conferencing for rural patients |
| Remote monitoring devices | BP, glucose, SpO2 monitors |
| Wearable integration | Activity, sleep, heart rate tracking |
| Patient engagement software | Chronic disease management apps |

### Partnership Model

```
USDA DLT Grant Structure:

Rural Health Partner (Lead Applicant)
├── Critical Access Hospital OR
├── Rural Health Clinic OR
├── Federally Qualified Health Center OR
└── Community Health Center

WellFit Community (Technology Partner)
├── Provides: Platform, devices, training
├── Receives: Equipment/software funding
└── Delivers: Telehealth + monitoring services
```

### Application Link

[USDA DLT Program](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/telecommunications-programs/distance-learning-telemedicine-grants)

## 2. HRSA Rural Health Network Development Program

### Program Overview

**Agency:** HRSA Federal Office of Rural Health Policy
**Program:** Rural Health Network Development Program (Network Development)

Supports integrated healthcare networks that work together to address health care needs in rural communities through formal collaborative arrangements.

### Funding Details

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Award Amount** | Up to $300,000/year |
| **Project Period** | 3 years |
| **Total Available** | ~$13 million annually |
| **FY 2026 Deadline** | Expected Spring/Summer 2026 |

### Eligible Applicants

- Public or private non-profit entities
- For-profit entities
- Faith-based organizations
- Tribes and tribal organizations

**Key Requirement:** Network must include at least 3 separately owned healthcare entities

### Recommended Network Members

HRSA recommends diverse networks including:
- Critical Access Hospitals
- Rural Health Clinics
- Community Health Centers/FQHCs
- Behavioral health organizations
- Social service organizations
- Colleges and universities
- Tribal organizations

### Why This Fits WellFit

| Program Goal | WellFit Solution |
|--------------|------------------|
| Integrate healthcare services | Platform connects multiple providers |
| Coordinate care | Care coordination tools |
| Address rural health needs | Remote monitoring reduces travel |
| Build sustainable networks | Shared technology infrastructure |

### Partnership Model

```
Rural Health Network Structure:

Network Lead (Fiscal Agent)
├── Rural hospital or FQHC

Network Members (3+ required)
├── Rural Health Clinic #1
├── Rural Health Clinic #2
├── Community Mental Health Center
├── Area Agency on Aging
└── WellFit Community (Technology Partner)

Shared Services via WellFit Platform:
├── Telehealth across all sites
├── Remote patient monitoring
├── Care coordination
├── Shared patient data (FHIR)
└── Population health analytics
```

### Application Link

[HRSA Network Development](https://www.hrsa.gov/grants/find-funding/HRSA-23-030)

## 3. HRSA Rural Health Network Development Planning

### Program Overview

**Agency:** HRSA Federal Office of Rural Health Policy
**Program:** Rural Health Network Development Planning Program

One-year planning grant for entities that don't have a history of formal collaborative efforts but want to develop an integrated healthcare network.

### Funding Details

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Award Amount** | Up to $100,000 |
| **Project Period** | 1 year |
| **Total Available** | ~$3 million annually |
| **FY 2026 Deadline** | Expected 2026 |

### Why This Fits WellFit

**Perfect for:** Rural partners who haven't worked together before and need planning time to:
- Define network governance
- Assess community needs
- Plan technology implementation
- Develop sustainability strategy

### Partnership Model

```
Planning Grant → Implementation Grant Pipeline:

Year 1: Planning Grant ($100K)
├── Community health needs assessment
├── Partner identification and MOUs
├── Technology assessment
├── Governance structure
└── Implementation plan

Years 2-4: Network Development Grant ($300K/year)
├── WellFit platform deployment
├── Device distribution
├── Care coordination launch
└── Outcome measurement
```

### Application Link

[HRSA Network Planning](https://www.hrsa.gov/grants/find-funding/HRSA-24-007)

## 4. HRSA Telehealth Network Grant Program

### Program Overview

**Agency:** HRSA Office for the Advancement of Telehealth
**Program:** Telehealth Network Grant Program (TNGP)

Supports telehealth networks that demonstrate how telehealth can improve healthcare access in rural and underserved communities.

### Funding Details

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Award Amount** | Up to $250,000/year |
| **Project Period** | 4 years |
| **FY 2026 Focus** | Tele-emergency, tele-stroke, tele-behavioral health |

### Current Priority Areas

1. **Tele-Emergency Services** - Rural ED consultation
2. **Tele-Stroke** - Rapid stroke assessment for rural hospitals
3. **Tele-Behavioral Health** - Mental health access in underserved areas
4. **Tele-EMS** - Emergency medical services support

### Why This Fits WellFit

| TNGP Priority | WellFit Capability |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Tele-Stroke | Neuro Suite with NIHSS calculator |
| Tele-Behavioral Health | PHQ-9, GAD-7, crisis detection, video therapy |
| Remote Monitoring | Vitals, wearables, chronic disease tracking |
| Care Coordination | Shared platform across network sites |

### Partnership Model

```
Telehealth Network Consortium:

Hub Site (Academic Medical Center or Large Hospital)
├── Provides: Specialist consultations
├── Receives: Referrals from spoke sites

Spoke Sites (Rural Providers)
├── Critical Access Hospitals
├── Rural Health Clinics
├── Community Health Centers

Technology Partner (WellFit)
├── Telehealth platform infrastructure
├── Remote monitoring integration
├── Care coordination tools
├── Training and support
```

### Application Link

[HRSA Telehealth Network](https://www.hrsa.gov/grants/find-funding/HRSA-20-036)

## 5. FCC Healthcare Connect Fund

### Program Overview

**Agency:** Federal Communications Commission
**Program:** Healthcare Connect Fund (part of Rural Health Care Program)

Provides 65% discount on telecommunications and broadband services for rural healthcare providers and consortia.

### Funding Details

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Discount Rate** | 65% of eligible costs |
| **Eligible Services** | Internet, data services, network construction |
| **Consortium Benefit** | Can include non-rural sites if majority rural |
| **Annual Cap** | ~$682 million program-wide |

### Eligible Entities

- Post-secondary educational institutions (health care instruction)
- Teaching hospitals and medical schools
- Community health centers
- Local health departments
- Community mental health centers
- Not-for-profit hospitals
- Rural health clinics
- Skilled nursing facilities
- Consortia of eligible providers

### Why This Fits WellFit

| Challenge | FCC Solution |
|-----------|--------------|
| Rural broadband costs | 65% discount on connectivity |
| Network infrastructure | Funding for dedicated healthcare networks |
| Consortium formation | Enables multi-site deployments |

### Partnership Model

```
Healthcare Consortium for FCC Funding:

Consortium Lead
├── Large health system OR
├── State rural health association

Consortium Members (majority rural)
├── Rural Hospital A
├── Rural Health Clinic B
├── FQHC C
├── Community Mental Health D
└── Skilled Nursing Facility E

Technology Deployment
├── Broadband connectivity (65% funded)
├── WellFit platform (operational costs)
└── Integrated telehealth services
```

### Application Link

[FCC Healthcare Connect Fund](https://www.fcc.gov/general/rural-health-care-program)

**Program:** Technology-Enabled Collaborative Learning Program (TELCP)

## Recommended Strategy: USDA DLT Grant Partnership

### Why USDA DLT is the Best Fit

1. **Direct technology funding** - Buys equipment and software
2. **Partnership model** - Rural health lead + technology partner
3. **Predictable timeline** - Annual funding cycle
4. **15% match** - Lower than many federal programs
5. **3-year period** - Time for sustainable implementation

### Ideal Rural Partners

| Partner Type | Why Good Fit |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Critical Access Hospital** | Medicare designation, established patient base |
| **FQHC** | Mission-aligned, underserved focus |
| **Rural Health Clinic** | Primary care base, chronic disease patients |
| **Tribal Health Facility** | Priority consideration, distinct needs |

### Geographic Targeting

States with strong rural health infrastructure:
- Texas (large rural population)
- Montana, Wyoming, Dakotas (frontier areas)
- Mississippi, Alabama (underserved South)
- Appalachian states (healthcare deserts)
- Tribal nations (IHS facilities)
