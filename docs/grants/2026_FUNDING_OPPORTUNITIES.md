## 2. CMS ACCESS Model

### Advancing Chronic Care with Effective, Scalable Solutions

**Status:** ✅ ACTIVE - Application drafted

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Agency** | CMS Innovation Center (CMMI) |
| **Type** | Voluntary Payment Model |
| **Deadline** | **April 1, 2026** (for July 2026 start) |
| **Duration** | 10 years (July 2026 - June 2036) |
| **Payments** | Outcomes-aligned, recurring technology payments |

**Model Details:**
- Covers technology costs for treating chronic conditions
- CMS waives patient co-payments for services
- Rewards clinical results, not specific services
- Rolling applications accepted through January 2033

**Covered Conditions:**
1. ✅ Diabetes
2. ✅ Hypertension
3. ✅ Chronic kidney disease
4. ✅ Obesity
5. ✅ Depression and anxiety
6. ✅ Chronic musculoskeletal pain
7. ✅ Related cardiometabolic disorders

**Why This Fits:**
- Technology-enabled care platform ✅
- Remote monitoring capabilities ✅
- Chronic condition management ✅ (all 7 conditions supported)
- Telehealth platform ✅
- Wearable integration ✅
- Patient engagement tools ✅

**Industry Interest:** 500+ organizations have submitted intent to apply

**Companion Initiative:** FDA TEMPO Pilot (Technology-Enabled Meaningful Patient Outcomes) launched December 2025 for accelerated digital health device approval

**Eligibility:** Medicare Part B-enrolled providers/suppliers (digital health companies can participate)

**Source:** [CMS ACCESS Model](https://www.cms.gov/priorities/innovation/innovation-models/access)

## Important Notes

### NIH SBIR/STTR Expired
The NIH Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) programs **expired October 1, 2025** per NOT-OD-26-006. NSF SBIR remains an alternative.

### FDA TEMPO Pilot
The FDA launched the Technology-Enabled Meaningful Patient Outcomes (TEMPO) pilot in December 2025 in coordination with CMS ACCESS Model. This accelerates digital health device approvals for chronic conditions.
