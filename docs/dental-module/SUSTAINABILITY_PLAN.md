# Dental Health Module: Sustainability Plan

## Executive Summary

This **Sustainability Plan** outlines how the **WellFit Dental Health Module** will achieve **long-term financial viability** and **programmatic continuity** beyond initial grant funding. Our strategy leverages **multiple revenue streams, strategic partnerships, operational efficiencies, and policy advocacy** to ensure the dental module becomes a **permanent, self-sustaining component** of the WellFit ecosystem.

**Bottom Line**: By Year 2, the dental module will generate **$1.1M-$2.35M in annual revenue** against **$75K in operating costs**, achieving **1,367% ROI** and full financial independence from grant funding.

### Cost Efficiency Gains

**Economies of Scale**:
- Year 1: $75K operating cost ÷ 1,000 patients = **$75/patient**
- Year 3: $120K operating cost ÷ 2,500 patients = **$48/patient** (36% reduction per patient)

**Operational Efficiencies**:
1. **Automated FHIR integration**: Reduces manual data entry by 80% (saves 10 hours/week staff time)
2. **Patient self-tracking**: Offloads routine monitoring from providers → More patients per FTE
3. **Telehealth triage**: CHWs handle 60% of dental concerns via virtual consult → Reduces in-person visits
4. **Shared equipment**: Partner with local dental clinic for X-ray access (eliminates $40K equipment purchase)

## 4. Partnership Development

### Strategic Partnerships for Sustainability

#### Clinical Partnerships

**Dental Schools**:
- **Partnership**: Dental hygiene students complete clinical rotations at WellFit
- **Value to WellFit**: Free/low-cost hygienist services (students supervised by licensed hygienist)
- **Value to Partner**: Community-based training experience
- **Sustainability Impact**: Reduces personnel costs by **$20K-$30K annually**

**Specialist Referral Network**:
- **Partnership**: Negotiate discounted rates with 5 specialists (periodontist, endodontist, oral surgeon, orthodontist, prosthodontist)
- **Value to WellFit**: 20-30% fee reduction for referrals
- **Value to Partner**: Guaranteed patient volume (100+ referrals/year)
- **Sustainability Impact**: Makes specialty care affordable for uninsured patients → Improves outcomes → Better performance metrics → Higher value-based care bonuses

#### Payer Partnerships

**Medicare Advantage Plans**:
- **Current**: 2 MA plans contract with WellFit
- **Growth Strategy**: Expand to 5 MA plans by Year 2
- **Negotiation Lever**: Demonstrate **HEDIS/Stars rating improvement** (Dental Services measure, HbA1c Control measure)
- **Revenue Impact**: Each new MA contract adds **$75K-$150K annually**

**Medicaid Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)**:
- **Partnership**: Propose **capitated payment model** ($200-$300 per member per year for comprehensive dental-medical integration)
- **Pilot**: Start with 200 high-risk diabetic/cardiac patients
- **Sustainability Impact**: Predictable revenue stream; incentivizes prevention over episodic care

#### Community Partnerships

**Area Agencies on Aging (AAAs)**:
- **Partnership**: AAAs refer seniors for dental screening via WellFit CHWs
- **Value to WellFit**: Free patient outreach and enrollment
- **Value to Partner**: Addresses social isolation and nutrition (linked to dental health)
- **Sustainability Impact**: Reduces patient acquisition cost to **$0**

**Food Banks**:
- **Partnership**: Co-locate dental screenings at monthly food distribution events
- **Value to WellFit**: Access to high-need population
- **Value to Partner**: Holistic service to clients (nutrition counseling includes dental health)
- **Sustainability Impact**: Identifies 50-100 new patients/year at **zero marketing cost**

## 5. Policy & Advocacy

### Policy Goals (Years 1-3)

#### State-Level Advocacy

**Goal 1: Medicaid Adult Dental Coverage Expansion**
- **Current Gap**: [Insert state] Medicaid covers dental only for children; adults get emergency-only care
- **Advocacy Strategy**:
  - Partner with state dental association to lobby state legislature
  - Share WellFit data showing **ED visit reduction** and **chronic disease improvement** from preventive dental care
  - Propose pilot: $5M state investment → 10,000 Medicaid adults receive preventive dental care → Estimated $8M savings from avoided complications
- **Timeline**: Legislative session Year 2; policy change by Year 3
- **Sustainability Impact**: Opens **$500K-$1M annual revenue** from expanded Medicaid coverage

**Goal 2: CHW Reimbursement for Dental Screening**
- **Current Gap**: CHWs not reimbursed for oral health screening
- **Advocacy Strategy**: Propose new **Medicaid billing code** for CHW oral health assessment (similar to SBIRT for substance use)
- **Sustainability Impact**: **$50-$100 per screening** → $50K-$100K additional annual revenue

#### Federal Advocacy

**Goal 3: Medicare Dental Benefit**
- **Current**: Medicare does not cover routine dental care
- **Advocacy Strategy**: Join **national coalition** (e.g., Oral Health America, Medicare Rights Center) advocating for dental coverage in Medicare
- **Timeline**: Long-term (5-10 years), but position WellFit as **demonstration site** if pilot program funded
- **Sustainability Impact**: Would unlock **$500K-$2M annually** if dental becomes Medicare-covered benefit

**Goal 4: CMS Innovation Center Model**
- **Opportunity**: Apply for **CMS Innovation Center grant** for "Medical-Dental Integration Demonstration"
- **Funding**: $2M-$5M over 3 years
- **Sustainability Impact**: Federal validation → Easier to secure state/foundation funding → Positions WellFit for national replication

## 6. Workforce Development

### Building a Dental Workforce Pipeline

#### Dental Hygienist Recruitment & Retention

**Challenge**: National shortage of dental hygienists; rural/underserved areas especially affected

**Strategy**:
1. **Partner with dental hygiene schools**: Offer student loan repayment assistance ($5K/year for 3-year commitment)
2. **Create career ladder**: Entry-level hygienist → Senior hygienist → Clinical supervisor → Dental program director
3. **Competitive compensation**: Start at **$60K** (above regional average of $55K)
4. **Professional development**: Annual conference attendance + 20 CEU hours paid

**Retention Goal**: 90% retention rate (vs. 70% industry average)

#### CHW Oral Health Champions

**Model**: Train **5 "Oral Health Champion" CHWs** to:
- Conduct basic oral health screenings (visual inspection, dry mouth check, pain assessment)
- Provide patient education (brushing/flossing technique, nutrition counseling)
- Coordinate dental referrals
- Track patient engagement with dental module

**Training**: 40-hour curriculum developed with state dental association (includes certification)

**Sustainability Impact**: CHWs cost **$20/hour** vs. hygienists at **$30/hour** → 33% cost savings for routine tasks

#### Dental Student Rotations

**Partnership**: University of [State] School of Dentistry
- **Structure**: 4th-year dental students complete 2-week community dentistry rotation at WellFit
- **Supervision**: Licensed dentist (volunteer or adjunct faculty)
- **Student Services**: Comprehensive exams, treatment planning, patient education
- **Sustainability Impact**: Free clinical services valued at **$15K-$30K annually**

## 7. Technology Scalability

**Current**: Supabase PostgreSQL (scales from 100 to 100,000 patients without re-architecture)

**Cost Scaling**:
- 1,000 patients: $50/month Supabase Pro plan
- 10,000 patients: $200/month Supabase Team plan
- 100,000 patients: $500/month Enterprise plan

**Benefit**: FHIR-compliant APIs enable integration with:

**Year 2-3 Development**: Generalize WellFit Dental Module into **configurable white-label solution**
- Customizable branding (logo, colors, terminology)
- Multi-tenant architecture (each health system has isolated database)
- Self-service admin portal (partners manage own users, workflows)

**Revenue Model**:
- **SaaS subscription**: $3,000-$5,000/month per health system
- **Implementation fee**: $25,000 one-time
- **Support**: $500/month for tier-2 technical support

**Year 5 Projection**: 10 licensees × $4K/month = **$480K annual recurring revenue**

## 8. Risk Mitigation

### Key Risks & Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Reimbursement rate cuts** (Medicaid/Medicare) | Medium | High | Diversify revenue streams; build reserves; advocate for rate increases |
| **Provider burnout** (hygienist turnover) | Medium | Medium | Competitive pay, career development, manageable caseloads |
| **Patient engagement drops** | Low | Medium | Gamification, SMS reminders, CHW outreach, culturally tailored education |
| **Technology failure** (data breach, downtime) | Low | High | HIPAA-compliant infrastructure, redundant backups, cyber insurance ($5K/year) |
| **Policy setback** (no Medicaid expansion) | Medium | Medium | Focus on federal opportunities (CMS Innovation), value-based contracts with existing payers |
| **Competition** (other dental integration models) | Medium | Low | Differentiate via FHIR compliance, patient-facing tech, health equity focus |

### Financial Reserves

**Goal**: Build 6-month operating reserve by Year 3
- **Target**: $37,500 (0.5 × $75K annual operating cost)
- **Funding**: Set aside **10% of Year 1-2 net revenue** annually
- **Purpose**: Buffer against reimbursement delays, policy changes, or unexpected costs
