# Garmin — Setup Guide

Connect your Garmin watch to WellFit so your doctor can see your heart rate, activity, sleep, and stress data.

## What Gets Shared

| Data | How Often |
|------|-----------|
| Heart rate (resting) | Daily |
| Blood oxygen (SpO2) | Daily (if supported by your model) |
| Steps, distance, calories | Daily |
| Sleep duration and stages | Each night |
| Stress level | Daily |
| Body composition | When measured (Garmin Index scale) |

## What You Need

- A **Garmin Connect account** (the one you use with the Garmin Connect app)
- Your Garmin device **synced to the Garmin Connect app** on your phone
- A phone, tablet, or computer with internet

## Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Make Sure Your Garmin Is Working

1. Open the **Garmin Connect** app on your phone
2. Check that your watch shows as connected and synced
3. Verify your daily stats (steps, heart rate) are appearing

### Step 2: Connect Garmin to WellFit

1. Log in to **WellFit**
2. Go to **My Devices** → **Connect a Wearable**
3. Tap **"Garmin"**
4. You'll be taken to Garmin's website to **sign in**
5. Garmin will ask you to authorize WellFit — tap **"Authorize"**
6. You'll be sent back to WellFit with a green checkmark

### Step 3: Verify It Works

1. Go to **My Health** → **Vitals**
2. You should see your latest heart rate, steps, and sleep data
3. If the data isn't there yet, wait **1 hour** for the first sync

## How It Works After Setup

- Your Garmin syncs to the Garmin Connect app as usual
- WellFit pulls your data from Garmin's servers
- Garmin also sends **real-time notifications** when new data is available
- Your doctor sees your daily readings and trends

## Supported Garmin Devices

Most Garmin watches with health features work, including:
- **Venu** series (Venu 2, Venu 3, Venu Sq)
- **vivoactive** series
- **Forerunner** series (245, 255, 265, 965)
- **fenix** series
- **Garmin Index** smart scale

## Troubleshooting

### "I don't see my data"
- Sync your Garmin in the Garmin Connect app
- Wait up to 1 hour
- Check **My Devices** for connection status

### "Authorization failed"
- Make sure you're signing in with the correct Garmin account
- Try again — Garmin's servers can be slow sometimes

## For Caregivers

- Help with the one-time authorization (Step 2)
- The senior just wears their Garmin as usual after setup
- Garmin watches are great for seniors — long battery life, always-on heart rate
