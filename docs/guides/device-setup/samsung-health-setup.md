# Samsung Galaxy Watch — Setup Guide

Connect your Samsung Galaxy Watch to WellFit so your doctor can see your heart rate, blood pressure, ECG, and activity data.

## What Gets Shared

| Data | Devices |
|------|---------|
| Heart rate | All Galaxy Watches |
| Blood pressure | Galaxy Watch Active2+, Galaxy Watch4+ |
| Blood oxygen (SpO2) | Galaxy Watch3+, Galaxy Watch4+ |
| ECG | Galaxy Watch Active2+, Galaxy Watch4+ |
| Steps, distance, calories | All Galaxy Watches |
| Sleep duration and stages | All Galaxy Watches |

## What You Need

- A **Samsung account** (the one you use with Samsung Health)
- Your Galaxy Watch **paired and syncing** with the Samsung Health app
- An **Android phone** (Samsung Health requires Android)
- A phone, tablet, or computer with internet

## Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Make Sure Your Galaxy Watch Is Working

1. Open the **Samsung Health** app on your Android phone
2. Check that your watch shows as connected
3. Verify your daily data (steps, heart rate) is appearing

### Step 2: Connect Samsung Health to WellFit

1. Log in to **WellFit**
2. Go to **My Devices** → **Connect a Wearable**
3. Tap **"Samsung Health"**
4. You'll be taken to Samsung's website to **sign in**
5. Samsung will ask: "Allow WellFit to read your health data?" — tap **"Allow"**
6. You'll be sent back to WellFit with a green checkmark

### Step 3: Verify It Works

1. Go to **My Health** → **Vitals**
2. You should see your latest readings
3. If the data isn't there yet, wait **1 hour** for the first sync

## Blood Pressure on Galaxy Watch

The Galaxy Watch can measure blood pressure, but it needs to be **calibrated** with a traditional cuff first:
1. Open the **Samsung Health Monitor** app on your watch
2. Follow the calibration steps (requires a regular blood pressure cuff)
3. Recalibrate every **4 weeks** for accuracy

## For Caregivers

- Help with the one-time sign-in (Step 2)
- Help calibrate the blood pressure feature if applicable
- The Galaxy Watch4 and newer are especially good for seniors — blood pressure, ECG, and fall detection built in
