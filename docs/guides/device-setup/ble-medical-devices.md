# Bluetooth Medical Devices — Setup Guide

This guide helps you connect Bluetooth medical devices to WellFit so your health readings are shared with your doctor automatically.

## Supported Devices

| Device Type | What It Measures | Example Brands |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Blood Pressure Cuff** | Blood pressure, heart rate | Omron, A&D, Beurer |
| **Glucometer** | Blood sugar (glucose) | OneTouch, Contour Next, ReliOn |
| **Pulse Oximeter** | Oxygen level, heart rate | Masimo, Nonin, Zacurate |
| **Smart Scale** | Weight, BMI | Withings, Eufy, Renpho |
| **Thermometer** | Body temperature | Braun, iHealth, Kinsa |

**Important:** Your device must have **Bluetooth** (look for the Bluetooth symbol on the box or device). Not all devices have Bluetooth — ask your pharmacist or caregiver to help you pick one that does.

## Step-by-Step: Connecting Your Device

### Step 1: Turn on Bluetooth on Your Phone or Computer

**On a phone (Android):**
1. Swipe down from the top of the screen
2. Tap the **Bluetooth** icon to turn it on (it should be blue)

**On a computer (Chrome browser):**
1. Bluetooth should already be on
2. If not, check your computer's settings

### Step 2: Turn On Your Medical Device

1. **Turn on your device** (press the power button)
2. Put it in **pairing mode** — usually this means holding the Bluetooth button for 3-5 seconds until a light blinks
3. Check your device's instruction booklet if you're not sure how

### Step 3: Open WellFit and Connect

1. **Log in to WellFit** in your Chrome browser
2. Go to **My Devices** (or ask your caregiver to navigate there)
3. Tap **"Connect a Device"**
4. Choose your device type (Blood Pressure, Glucometer, etc.)
5. A popup will appear asking to pair — **select your device** from the list
6. Wait for the green checkmark — you're connected!

### Step 4: Take a Reading

1. Use your device as you normally would (wrap the cuff, insert the strip, clip the oximeter, etc.)
2. Your reading will **automatically appear in WellFit** within a few seconds
3. Your doctor will see it on their dashboard

## Taking Readings — Tips for Seniors

### Blood Pressure
- Sit quietly for **5 minutes** before taking a reading
- Keep your arm at **heart level** on a table
- Don't talk during the reading
- Take readings at the **same time each day** (morning is best)

### Blood Sugar (Glucose)
- Wash your hands with warm water before testing
- Use the **side of your fingertip**, not the very tip
- Record whether you tested **before or after a meal**

### Pulse Oximeter
- Sit still and keep your hand **warm** (cold fingers give bad readings)
- Remove **nail polish** — it can affect the reading
- Wait **10 seconds** for the reading to stabilize

### Weight
- Weigh yourself at the **same time each day** (morning, before eating)
- Stand still on the scale with both feet flat
- Wear similar clothing each time

### Temperature
- Wait **15 minutes** after eating or drinking before taking your temperature
- Follow your thermometer's instructions for forehead, ear, or oral readings

## Troubleshooting

### "I don't see my device in the list"
- Make sure your device is **turned on** and in **pairing mode**
- Make sure **Bluetooth is on** on your phone or computer
- Move your device **closer** (within 3 feet)
- Try **turning your device off and on** again

### "The reading didn't show up"
- Wait **30 seconds** — sometimes it takes a moment
- Make sure you're still on the WellFit page
- Try taking another reading

### "I get an error message"
- Close the browser and open it again
- Make sure you're using **Google Chrome** (not Safari or Firefox)
- Ask your caregiver to help

### "My device disconnected"
- This is normal — the device disconnects after sending the reading
- It will reconnect automatically next time you take a reading
- If it doesn't reconnect, go to **My Devices** and tap **"Reconnect"**

## For Caregivers

### First-Time Setup
1. Help the senior log in to WellFit on their device (phone, tablet, or computer)
2. Use **Google Chrome** — Bluetooth does not work in Safari or Firefox
3. Navigate to the device setup page and pair the device
4. Take one test reading to confirm it works
5. Show the senior where to see their readings

### Ongoing Support
- The device remembers the pairing — the senior just needs to take readings
- If they get a new phone or computer, you'll need to pair again
- Check the WellFit dashboard periodically to make sure readings are coming through

### Offline Readings
If the senior takes a reading when they don't have internet:
- The reading is saved locally on the device
- It will sync to WellFit the next time they open the app with internet
- Up to **500 readings** are stored offline

## Which Devices Work Best?

For seniors, we recommend devices that are:
- **Large display** — easy to read
- **One-button operation** — simple to use
- **Bluetooth enabled** — sends readings automatically
- **FDA-cleared** — accurate and reliable

Ask your doctor or pharmacist for specific brand recommendations.
