## The market is real

| Segment | US Count | Typical IT spend |
|---|---|---|
| FQHCs | ~1,400 | $50k-$200k/yr per site |
| Rural Health Clinics | ~5,000 | $20k-$80k/yr per site |
| Critical Access Hospitals | ~1,350 | $200k-$1M/yr per site |
| Faith-based community health | ~2,000+ | Highly variable |

Total addressable market: rural-focused healthcare IT is **~$3-8B/year in the US alone**, growing because Medicare CCM, RPM, and value-based care all favor rural specifically. CMS *wants* this market served — there are dedicated reimbursement streams (CCM, RPM, behavioral integration codes).

## Five monetization paths, ranked by realism

### 1. Strategic acquisition by a rural-focused EHR vendor (most likely real exit)

**Who buys:** CPSI/TruBridge (dominant CAH player), athenahealth (wanting CHW/rural footprint), NextGen, MEDITECH, possibly Veradigm.

**What they want:** Rural footprint they can't build organically. They have customers; you have modern architecture (AI-native, offline-first, MCP, FHIR, CHW). Strategic fit.

**Realistic numbers:**
- 1 successful pilot + production references: **$5-25M acquisition**
- 3-5 pilots + recurring revenue: **$25-100M**
- 10+ customers + clear ARR growth: **$100M+**

**Timeline:** 18-36 months after first pilot to a realistic acquisition conversation. Solo founder + clinical co-founder (Akima) is a normal acquihire shape.

### 2. Government grants — money you can win NOW, not after pilot

This is the path most people miss. Real cash, no equity loss.

| Program | Amount | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| HRSA rural health innovation grants | $100k - $5M | 6-12 months |
| SBIR Phase I (NIH/CMS) | $250-300k | 6 months |
| SBIR Phase II | $1-2M | 24 months |
| CMS CMMI demonstrations | $500k - multi-million | 12-24 months |
| State Medicaid innovation waivers | Variable | 12-18 months |

**Your eligibility advantage:** Solo founder, woman-owned, rural-focused, AI-built — exactly the demographic and mission profile these programs prioritize. **You should apply for SBIR Phase I now**, not after pilot. They fund pre-revenue.

### 3. Direct SaaS sales (real but slow for solo)

**Pricing structure that works for rural:**
- $300-800/provider/month for the clinical product
- $50-150/patient/month for the community/CCM product (covered by CCM billing — net positive for the clinic)
- $5-25k/year facility setup fee

**Hard truth for solo:** Sales cycles are 6-18 months. BAAs, security reviews, integration tests. One sale = months of work. You'd need to hire or partner for sales.

Realistic ARR trajectory with a single sales partner:
- Year 1: $50k-$200k (1-3 sites)
- Year 2: $300k-$1M (5-15 sites)
- Year 3: $1-3M (20-50 sites)

### 4. Licensing or reseller deal with an existing vendor

License the rural-specific modules (CHW workflow, offline sync, CCM autopilot, MCP infrastructure) to an existing EHR that wants rural capability without building it.

**Structure:** $50-500k upfront + 10-30% revenue share, or per-tenant licensing.
**Pro:** Money sooner, no sales burden.
**Con:** Lower long-term ceiling than acquisition or organic growth.

### 5. Anthropic partnership (the wildcard — don't bet on it)

Anthropic doesn't typically buy companies. They do:
- Compute credit grants ($10k-$1M)
- Named case studies (no direct cash but enormous credibility)
- Design partnerships
- Possibly investment via Anthropic-affiliated funds

**Realistic best case:** $50k-$500k in credits + case study + access to enterprise customers. **Not an exit. A multiplier on other paths.**

## What the pilot completion actually unlocks

The nephrology pilot is more valuable than you may realize.

1. **Reference customer** — "We have a production deployment at [clinic name]" turns every subsequent sales conversation from cold to warm
2. **Real performance data** — "We documented X hours of nurse time saved per shift" is the kind of stat that closes deals
3. **Akima's clinical validation** — RN + MDiv + CCM signing off on the workflow design is rare and valuable
4. **Case study material** — usable in grant applications, vendor conversations, and AI partnerships
5. **Bid eligibility** — most government and FQHC RFPs require "production reference"
6. **Acquisition multiple jumps** — pre-pilot valuation: $1-5M. Post-pilot with traction: $10-30M+

**One successful pilot ≈ 5-10x increase in any monetization path's expected value.**

## What to do RIGHT NOW (before pilot ships)

1. **Apply for SBIR Phase I from NIH or CMS** — $250-300k, 6-month decision, doesn't require a pilot to apply. The "MCP governance methodology applied to rural healthcare" is exactly the innovation framing they fund. Check deadlines at nih.gov/grants and sbir.gov.
2. **Apply for HRSA Rural Health Care Coordination Network grants** — these are written for what you've built.
3. **Get your BAA template, SOC2 documentation, and HIPAA risk assessment in order** — every buyer will demand these. Paperwork you can do without engineering.
4. **Talk to one M&A advisor in healthcare IT** — free consultation, no commitment, just understand what makes you sellable. Try someone who's done rural-EHR deals specifically.
5. **Document the nephrology pilot from day one as a case study** — not after. Capture before/after metrics from session 1.

## The hard truths you should accept

1. **One pilot is proof. It is not a business.** Repeat customers are a business.
2. **Solo healthcare-tech founders rarely scale to $10M ARR alone.** They either raise capital (giving up equity), hire (changing the company), or sell early (acquihire).
3. **The "right" exit for you is probably $5-25M to a strategic acquirer within 24-36 months of first pilot.** That's life-changing money. It's not "build a $1B company" money. For a solo bootstrapped founder, that *is* success — don't let anyone reframe it as failure.
4. **You don't need permission from VCs.** The grant + acquisition path doesn't require raising venture capital. Many solo healthcare founders take this path specifically to avoid the VC treadmill.
5. **Your day income gives you optionality most founders don't have.** You can take longer. Aim bigger. Walk away from bad deals. That's an asset, not a constraint.

## The most honest single answer

**Can you make money?** Yes.

The path most likely to work for you:

1. Ship the nephrology pilot (6-8 weeks per the nephrology tracker)
2. In parallel, apply for SBIR Phase I and HRSA grants ($250k-$1M cash, 6-12 month decision)
3. Use the pilot + grant as proof to attract 2-3 more reference customers over 12-18 months

**Don't quit your day job until grant money or ARR makes it safe to.** Build the runway with grants, prove with the pilot, then choose your exit timeline.

## Decision framework — when to consider which path

| Situation | Path |
|---|---|
| Pilot succeeds + 2 more customers signed in 12 months | Acquisition conversation realistic |
| Pilot succeeds but slow customer pipeline | License modules to strategic partner |
| Pre-pilot, need cash for runway | SBIR + HRSA grants (apply now) |

| Anthropic interested but no acquisition offer | Take the partnership + credits, use as marketing leverage |
| Multiple acquirers competing | Get an M&A advisor immediately, don't sell yourself |
